## [Agora: Enhancing LLM Agent Reasoning Via Auction-Based Task Allocation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09600v1)
**Score:** 0.924  |  **arXiv:** 2607.09600v1
**Length:** 12 pages

To address the limitations of coarse task-routing in multi-LLM systems, this paper introduces **Agora**, a framework that uses an auction-based mechanism to dynamically allocate reasoning steps to the most capable and cost-effective expert models. By forcing models to bid based on their calibrated competence, Agora prevents overconfident but less-capable models from taking on tasks they cannot solve. Evaluation across five benchmarks shows that the framework outperforms traditional routing and cascade baselines while offering a highly controllable cost-quality trade-off.

## [PAC-ACT: Post-training Actor-Critic for Action Chunking Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09590v1)

By optimizing policies at the chunk level using a hybrid behavior-prior constraint, the method mitigates the distribution shift issues of standard behavior cloning while maintaining low inference latency and memory usage. Evaluation on industrial benchmarks demonstrates that PAC-ACT significantly improves task success and contact stability, dramatically reducing peak contact forces during manipulation.
